Leadership Lab 2017 & 2018 Campaigns

Project Challenge: Create a comprehensive campaign around a summer program involving up to 30 youth for up to 9 weeks.

In 2017, project activities were centered around the following goals:

- Build Social Media presence and consistency to establish routines, infrastructure and to gather data.
- Connect to influencer accounts for more top-of-mind relationship among summer partners.
- Meet Canada 150 funding and external organization joint campaign requirements.
- Delegate team duties such that they support respective neighbourhood teams in their media and promotional needs as determined by each team.

Results and insights drawn from that project included:

- A better understanding of the role of social media for our summer program, and which accounts to focus on.
- The need for a new evaluation infrastructure to be built and employed (which I later incorporated into the program).
- The importance of limiting projects to more impactful content creation rather than mass content generation.
- The need for emphasis on empirical research to be built into project activities.
- The benefits of hiring more directly towards distinct roles rather than for general team members.

In 2018, project activities were centered around the following goals:

- Focus Social Media presence to build inter-program and external relationships rather than simple content generation.
- Directly approach influencers with content to coordinate tagging and content sharing efforts.
- Create fewer but larger projects than 2017 Leadership Lab due to their effectiveness in drawing in our contacts.
- More greatly integrate team members with neighbourhood teams such that they can more greatly generate media content.
- The above goals were drawn entirely from insights developed via the 2017 Lab as well as additional research.

Results and insights drawn from the 2018 project included:

- Allowing Communications Assistants to fully integrate with the teams rather than to operate as a single Communications Team was more
- Separating team duties into more specific roles allowed for greater depth to be achieved on a per-project basis due to specialization and skill development during the program.
- Setting Communications-Centric "Communications Days" and presentations allowed for the program participants to better understand the team's abilities and kept media creation top-of-mind
- More greatly emphasizing evaluation, reporting and data gathering has greatly increased our ability to prove program effectiveness.
- Explicitly encouraging direct outreach has greatly increased our interactions and cooperation with MPs and other external connections.

From these campaigns, I learned how to iterate and analyze my own plans in a formal setting. An important insight I drew from this is the importance of building in uncertainty and backup plans into long-term strategies. Another important learning point was to learn how to consider the logistic and intangible aspects of a project. Examples of such challenges were ensuring that employee hours were easily tracked, building in a self-reported payroll hours tracking system, and understanding that shuffling the order of different projects and checkpoints can help build team momentum by building off of previous skills gained.

  • Client: Trinity Theatre
  • Date: Summers of 2017 and 2018
  • Service: Video Editing

JISA Campaign:

Project Challenge: Develop relations with other clubs through joint events.

Key concerns from our exec team is that our club membership was outgrowing the executive team's abilities to keep them engaged, and a team expansion late in the year was expected to be difficult to properly initiate. In exploring these concerns, we realized that our membership had significant crossover with other similar clubs, and would attend their events during our team's downtime. This prompted us to take the following steps:

- Initiate contact with the YAMA Anime and Manga Club, Students for Board Games, and the Seneca Anime Club.
- Plan and book space for a board game's night with larger-than-usual attendants.
- Fulfill documentation and budgetary requirements, and coordinate resource splitting.
- Coordinate advertising among all four clubs to be consistent and frequent.
- Brainstorm and employ a split of resources and responsibilities among all clubs involved.
From this campaign, we ran a board games night that brought in over 90 attendees, the largest in at least 5 years of our club's history. This also built our connections within other York clubs, allowing us to generate greater attendance to future events for all clubs. By acknowledging the intuitive crossover of our membership bases, we were able to leverage the multiplicative effect of multiple exec teams and resource pools being focused at once. Funding reimbursements allowed the event to function as a fundraiser as well.

Because our club was the largest of the four with independent campus space, it established our club as a central meeting point for future events and reintegrated our historically semi-isolated club into the greater club network.
From this event I learned the importance of thinking outside my current resource pool to seek mutual benefit from other talented groups. I also developed my ability to coordinate with multiple unfamiliar individuals with this event, with a major focus on learning how to determine which responsibilities to delegate versus which ones to undertake independently.

  • Client: JISA
  • Date: December 2015
  • Service: Video Editing
